# AI Video Maker — How to Use It

Welcome! This is a private video-making site — you type what you want a video
about, and it writes a script, then turns it into a narrated video with
subtitles. Nobody outside the people invited by the owner can use it.

## Getting started

1. Go to the site's link (the owner will give you this).
2. Log in with the email and password the owner gave you.
3. If you ever forget your password, ask the owner — only they can create or
   reset accounts.

## Making your first video

1. Click **New video**.
2. Type a short description of what you want the video to be about — as
   much or as little detail as you like. Example: *"3 easy healthy
   breakfast ideas"*.
3. Pick a **language** (Hindi or English), a **length** (30s to 5 min), and a
   **format** (9:16 for reels/shorts, 16:9 for widescreen).
4. Click **Generate script**. In a few seconds you'll get a scene-by-scene
   script.
5. **Read it over.** You can:
   - Edit any line's text directly.
   - Select some text and ask the AI to improve just that part.
   - Scrap it and start over if you don't like it at all.
6. When you're happy with it, click **Accept & continue**. This step matters
   — nothing gets turned into a video until you explicitly accept the
   script.

## Turning your script into a video

After accepting, you'll choose how to bring it to life:

### Image Video (the easy, fast option)
Picks relevant photos for each scene, narrates your script over them with
gentle zoom/pan motion, adds subtitles, and (optionally) background music.
Ready in about 2 minutes.

Under **Visual quality** you'll usually see two choices:
- **Photo (fast)** — always available, ready in ~2 minutes.
- **Generated footage** — real AI-animated video clips instead of still
  photos, much more impressive, but only shows up as clickable when the
  owner's computer is on and connected. If it's greyed out, it's not your
  fault — just try again later, or ask the owner. When it *is* available,
  expect to wait 20–60 minutes; you can close the tab and check back.

### My Avatar
A talking video of *you* (or a persona you create) narrating the script.
See "Creating your AI avatar" below — you need to set one up first.

Either way, once you click **Generate video**, you'll land on a progress
page. It updates itself — no need to keep refreshing.

## When it's done

You'll see a video player, plus:
- **Download MP4 + credits** — the finished video, plus a text file
  crediting any stock photos/music used.
- **Make another** — start a fresh project.
- **Swap image** / **Re-render scene** — didn't like one particular scene's
  picture? Fix just that one without redoing the whole video.
- **Re-render as My Avatar** (or vice versa) — turn the same script into the
  other video style with one click.

## Making it sound like *you*

By default, videos are narrated in a stock voice. To have every video
narrated in **your own voice** instead:

1. On the video-options page, click **Enroll now** next to the voice
   section.
2. Read the short passage shown out loud, clearly, in a quiet room.
3. Once enrolled, every future video defaults to your voice automatically —
   no need to do anything differently.

## Creating your AI avatar

1. Go to **Avatars** → **New avatar**.
2. Upload a clear, front-facing photo of yourself.
3. Pick a persona (Astrologer, Businessman, Teacher, Doctor, News Anchor) or
   describe your own.
4. Check the consent box (confirming it's really you) and click
   **Create avatar**.
5. You'll be shown the result — **Approve** it, or **Regenerate** with a
   different description if you want to try again.
6. Once approved, it's reusable across every future video — you won't need
   to redo this step.

**Honest note**: styling only works while the owner's home GPU is online
(check the badge on the generate page). When it's online, your photo is
genuinely restyled into the persona you described. When it's offline,
you'll see and approve your own real, unedited photo instead — the avatar
video itself still works fine either way, it just won't look like the
fictional persona when the GPU is offline.

## A few things worth knowing

- **This is a private site for a small number of people.** There's no
  public sign-up — every account is created individually by the owner.
- **The site may occasionally be unavailable** if the owner's computer is
  off. If a page won't load, it's usually temporary — try again in a bit,
  or message the owner.
- **Everything is free to use**, with generous but not unlimited daily
  quotas shared across everyone invited. If you see a message about a daily
  limit being reached, it resets at midnight and isn't a sign anything is
  broken.
- **Videos are marked as AI-generated** in their file metadata, and nothing
  is posted anywhere automatically — you decide what to do with your
  downloads.

## Getting help

If something looks broken, isn't loading, or you're not sure what to do
next — just message the owner. They can see what's happening on their side
and fix it much faster than guessing from the outside.
